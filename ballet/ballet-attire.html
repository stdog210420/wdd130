Ballet is all about discipline, grace, elegance and strength.To do that, evey ballet studio has its own rules. However, there are some common senses for the attire. Subsequently, we will introduce how to wear comfortable, form-fitting clothing and proper shoes.

Wearing the Right Outfit

image credit: https://www.dance10.org/about/dress-code/ballet-classes/
Attribution-NonCommercial

Ballet attire

First, you can choose to wear a leggings with a cap sleeve or sleeveless tops shown as the above home image, or tights with a leotard and a sheer wrap skirt shown as the right image. The dress code is you have to make room for better flexibility, plus it also helps minimise the risk of sustaining injury by keeping the leg muscles warm. Moreover, it will make your teachers see your alignment, allowing them to correct the next steps compared to baggy clothes, hiding poor practices.

Second, don't wear jewelry or a wristwatch to dance class since it may fly off while you are moving and hit someone. Additionally, it impair technique.

Third, choose a suitable soft ballet shoes if you are a beginner.

Fourth, tie your hair secured back and off the face to avoid interfering with steps like pirouettes or jumps.

How to tie a basic ballet bun

image credit: https://danceworld.ie/blogs/news/how-to-make-a-perfect-ballet-bun
Attribution-NonCommercial

A ballet bun is quite simple to do. With a little practice, you will get the perfect “cinnamon bun”. 1. Brush your hair.To start, get your hair free of tangles. 2. Pull your hair back into a ponytail and secure with an elastic. 3. Twist the bun into a rope and wrap the rope in a circle around the elastic.Wrap the rope around in a circle around the elastic. 4. Use hairpins to secure the bun to your head. Secure the bun to your head using U-shaped hair pins not bobby pins to grab hair from the top of your bun. 5. Wrap the hair net around the bun and secure with more pins to secure all the loose hairs. Cover your bun with a hair net that matches your hair color to keep all wisps and stray hairs in place. 6. Use hairspray, gel, or bobby pins / clips to tame the loose hairs away from your face. For an extra hold and overall neat look, use hairspray to slick all the loose wisps or fizz from your hairline flat on the head.

How to buy shoes

image credit: https://movedancewear.com/blog/buying_the_right_ballet_shoe/54/
Attribution-NonCommercial

Ballet shoes play an important role for safely practicing ballet movements.
Opt for a pair of Ballet slippers rather than pointe shoes – they’re soft & easy for beginners to wear.
There are three kinds of materials to choose- satin, canvas, and leather.
Among them, canvas are the most popular ballet shoes; on the contrary, leather ones are more expensive but rather durable.
Satin ones are preferred for performances but the least durable.
